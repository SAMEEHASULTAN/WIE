Common Interview Questions

Tell me about yourself

Start with your current situation, then briefly cover your professional background, and end with why you're interested in this role. Keep it professional and relevant to the job.

Sample Answer:

"I'm a marketing professional with 8 years of experience, currently returning after a 2-year career break during which I upskilled in digital marketing. I'm excited to apply my refreshed skills in this role..."

Why did you take a career break?

Be honest but positive. Focus on what you did during the break and how it made you better prepared for work.

Sample Answer:

"I took time to focus on family, and during this period, I completed several online courses in [relevant skills]. This break has given me renewed energy and perspective..."

How do you handle skill gaps?

Show your willingness to learn and give examples of how you've successfully learned new skills in the past.

Sample Answer:

"I'm a quick learner and have already started updating my skills through online courses. I recently completed [course name] and I'm confident I can quickly master any new requirements..."
